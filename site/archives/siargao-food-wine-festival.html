Siargao Food & Wine Festival — Ani Sang Siargao

August 26–31, 2026
Culinary Festival | Island-Wide Program | 9 Municipalities, Siargao

In collaboration with

Wild Siargao

In Bisaya, ani means harvest. For 2026, the festival is built around the act of harvest — of the trees, the sea, the farms, the people, and the shared table.

Table Talks' Community Impact Fund is a partner of Ani Sang Siargao, the Siargao Food & Wine Festival, running August 26–31, 2026 across all nine municipalities of Siargao Island. Now in its second year, the festival is co-directed by Jove Schrottmann and Chef Andrew Malarky of Wild Siargao, and organised by Rude Boys Hospitality. Our 2025 inaugural edition proved the model. 2026 scales it.

Each day surfaces a different harvest of Siargao — Discovery, Ocean, Coconut, Producer, Community and Celebration — across 30+ culinary collaborators (chefs, carinderias, farmers, foragers, baristas and distillers), 8+ anchor events and 20+ pocket experiences.

The programme runs from an opening gala at Wild Siargao through a wet-market experience with Roots, a Coconut Lab at Lokal Hub, a full day of hands-on farming and foraging at Ihayas Farm, a community Mercado at Bravo Beach Resort, and a closing celebration at Hue Hotel overlooking the Cloud 9 surf break — with collaboration dinners bringing together chefs including Jarrod Moore, Morris Danzen, Hannah Sanchez, Dom Marquez-Hammond, Marc Silvestre Carbó and David Amoros alongside the Wild Siargao team.

"Food is for everybody, and I want everyone to be part of it — especially our local eateries." — Chef Andrew Malarky, Lead Organizer, Wild Siargao

The festival runs across two hubs: The Commons at Bravo Beach Resort, open and social, hosting the Mercado, talks and a wine garden; and The Hearth at Wild Siargao, intimate and culturally focused, hosting the opening gala, chef collaboration dinners and producer dinners.

In 2025, the festival's Instagram following grew 182%, with a 1,701% increase in impressions and 494% increase in engagement — 2.33M earned impressions and 1.94M combined reach, with coverage from Rappler, Lifestyle Asia, Philstar, MindaNews, SunStar Davao and Travel and Tour World.

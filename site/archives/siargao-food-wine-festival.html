Siargao Food & Wine Festival — Ani Sang Siargao

August 26–31, 2026
Culinary Festival | Island-Wide Program | 9 Municipalities, Siargao

In collaboration with

Wild Siargao

The Philippines' first community-led, island-wide culinary festival.

The Siargao Food and Wine Festival is a six-day celebration that brings together chefs, carinderia cooks, farmers, fishers, foragers, baristas and creatives across all nine municipalities of Siargao Island. Now entering its second year, the festival celebrates Siargao's evolving culinary identity while creating meaningful space for conversations on sustainability, regenerative tourism and community livelihoods.

Our 2025 inaugural edition proved the model. 2026 scales it — with stronger programming, deeper community integration, and a partnership architecture inspired by Terra Madre (Slow Food, Turin, Bacolod) and the Ubud Food Festival (Bali, Indonesia).

THE THEME

Ani Sang Siargao — The Harvest of Siargao

In Bisaya, ani means harvest. For 2026, the festival is built around the act of harvest — of the trees, the sea, the farms, the people, and the shared table. Each day surfaces a different harvest of Siargao. Each meal honors the hands behind it.

"Food is for everybody, and I want everyone to be part of it — especially our local eateries."

— Chef Andrew Malarky, Lead Organizer, Wild Siargao

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Festival experiences.

Opening at Wild

A high-energy celebration bringing together chefs, farmers, media, creatives and partners from across the island — cultural performances, music, cocktails and a collaborative chef-led dining experience.

Siargao Coffee Crawl

In its second year — a week of coffee tastings, roasting workshops and latte art competitions across the island's coffee culture.

Wet Market Experience by Roots

Set in the heart of the General Luna Wet Market, among fishermen and local vendors — fresh seafood, island drinks and an open, community-driven atmosphere.

Coconut Lab

From tree to table — workshops on processing, fermentation and zero-waste coconut utilization, featuring Chef Morris Danzen Catanghal and Chef Jarrod Moore.

Kinilaw & Vinegar Workshops

Siargao's seafood heritage on full display — kinilaw prepared the traditional way, with just-caught fish, sharp local vinegars and native acids, tasted in several regional variations.

Tropical Academy at Ihayas Farm

A full day immersed in island agriculture — hands-on farming, foraging and workshops with farmers and chefs, closing with a shared farm lunch celebrating the harvest.

Siargao Mercado at Bravo

Karinderyas, local eateries and Siargao's favourite restaurants gather by the beach — live performances, workshops and cultural programming for locals and visitors alike.

Closing Ceremonies at Hue Hotel

Overlooking the Cloud 9 surf break, Ani Sang Siargao concludes with an afternoon of recognition, celebration and community at the newly opened Hue Hotel.

Transportation partner: Doon PH and Jeepney Tours connect The Commons at Bravo Beach Resort and The Hearth at Wild Siargao, with day trips to Ihayas Farm and the General Luna Wet Market.

THE 6-DAY JOURNEY

Each day pulls a new harvest.

Discovery on day one. Harvest of the trees, the sea, the land, the people. Shared celebration to close.

DAY 1 — AUG 26

Opening + Discovery

The festival opens with the Opening Night Gala at Wild Siargao. Guests discover the breadth of Siargao's culinary landscape through a curated multi-chef collaboration menu, paired with natural wines and craft cocktails, while a Coffee & Spirits Night runs concurrently across partner venues.

DAY 2 — AUG 27

Coconut: Tree of Life

The Coconut Lab invites guests into hands-on workshops exploring processing, fermentation and zero-waste cooking — from fresh buko to aged lambanog. The evening continues with a Coconut Bar Takeover across partner venues.

DAY 3 — AUG 28

Bounty of the Ocean

The Kinilaw Open Championship is the festival's most spirited competitive event — local and visiting chefs crafting Siargao's beloved raw seafood dish, judged on freshness, technique and flavour. The evening shifts to the coast with Coastal Cocktails Night.

DAY 4 — AUG 29

Producer Day

A full day spent with the people who grow, catch and forage what Siargao eats — farm tours and foraging walks, culminating in a long lunch with the fisherfolk: a communal table set in nature, honouring the invisible backbone of every meal on the island.

DAY 5 — AUG 30

Community Day

Day 5 belongs to everyone. The Siargao Mercado at Bravo opens its gates — a public, free-entry market with local vendors, artisans and food producers from across the island's nine municipalities. The festival's most inclusive moment.

DAY 6 — AUG 31

Closing + Celebration

The festival closes as it began — with gratitude and ceremony. Sunset Closing Ceremonies at Hue gather chefs, farmers, partners, volunteers and guests, honouring the producers and karinderyas whose labour powered the harvest.

FESTIVAL ARCHITECTURE

A dual-hub island festival.

The Commons — Bravo Beach Resort

Open, social, free entry — the festival's accessible heart. Siargao Mercado and public talks, cultural performances and wine garden tastings, food festival and on-ground activations. 10,000+ projected foot traffic — locals, tourists, surfers, press and creators.

The Hearth — Wild Siargao

Intimate and culturally focused — the festival's culinary core. Opening gala and chef collaboration dinners, the Coconut Lab and kinilaw workshops, producer tasting dinners. Around 800 premium ticketed attendees — chefs, food media and premium travellers.

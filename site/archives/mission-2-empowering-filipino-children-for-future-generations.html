Mission 2: Empowering Filipino Children for Future Generations

General Luna, Siargao PH
Community Impact Fund | Alternative Education | General Luna, Siargao

In collaboration with

Wild Siargao & Hue Hotel

Filipinos are capable. Filipinos are creative. Filipinos are talented and resilient. What many communities lack is not ability. It's access.

This mission runs on Made by Many, the participation model behind every Table Talks Community Impact Fund project. Instead of separating audiences, clients, donors and communities, it believes people should get to help build the thing itself — Discover a story worth telling, Meet the Creators behind it, back it through Community Contributions, watch it move through Production, come together at a Gathering, and leave behind a Legacy that outlasts the event.

Our concrete first step was Mission 001, a fundraiser and meal distribution in General Luna, run in collaboration with Wild Siargao and Hue Hotel. As at this mission's most recent update: $125 raised, $125 distributed, 6 families supported, 1 community meal delivered — small, real numbers from a mission that's still just getting started.

The direction we're building toward is alternative education: expanding access to learning, creativity and future opportunity for Filipino children and young adults, through community workshops, creative learning, entrepreneurship and life-skills programmes — the kind of programme that lets a young person fund their own educational experiences, learn through local businesses and mentors, and see more of what's possible beyond their own barangay.

"Sometimes it's giving books to a child and encouraging them to dream bigger."

Community education partners for this track — including Espoir School of Life and Lokal Academy — are still being confirmed. We'll update this page once they're locked in.
